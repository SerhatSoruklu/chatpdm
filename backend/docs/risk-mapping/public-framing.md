# Public Framing

RMG is bounded risk mapping over authored domain artifacts.
It is not a prediction system, probability engine, or hidden-truth detector.

## What RMG Is

- bounded risk mapping
- deterministic admission, narrowing, or refusal
- supported structural path mapping
- authored evidence support inspection
- bounded support confidence classification
- inspectable and replayable output surfaces

## What RMG Is Not

- a prediction engine
- a probability engine
- an intelligence engine
- a forecasting system
- a hidden truth detector
- a system that knows what will happen

## Correct Public Framing

Use wording that stays inside authored support and explicit boundaries.

Preferred phrases:

- supported structural paths
- authored evidence support
- deterministic output
- bounded support confidence
- explicit boundary outcome
- inspectable audit surface
- replayable release state

## Prohibited Framing

Do not describe RMG as something that:

- predicts collapse
- forecasts the future
- knows what will happen
- detects hidden truth
- produces probabilities
- estimates odds or likelihood

## How to Describe Output Surfaces

- supported paths: supported structural paths within admitted scope
- confidence: bounded support confidence within authored constraints
- refusals: outside the current authored support boundary
- explain/audit: structural accounting for why the output was admitted, narrowed, or refused

## Safe Wording Examples

- "RMG returned a supported structural path within the admitted scope."
- "RMG classified bounded support confidence as medium under authored constraints."
- "RMG refused the query because it was outside the authored support boundary."
- "The audit surface shows the replayable authored artifacts used for the decision."

## Unsafe Wording Examples

- "RMG predicts collapse."
- "RMG forecasts the future."
- "RMG knows what will happen."
- "RMG detects hidden truth."
- "RMG is a probability engine."
- "RMG estimates the odds of collapse."
