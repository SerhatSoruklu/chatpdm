What it is

A protocol-grade enforcement boundary.

It evaluates a proposed action against a validated pack and decides whether execution is admissible. It does not invent meaning, infer missing facts, or defer final admissibility to upstream analysis.

Why this layer exists

Admissibility cannot be inferred at execution time.

Upstream planning, analysis, recommendation, and human-proposal surfaces can produce candidate actions. They cannot establish final admissibility under constraint.

Upstream systems may produce plausible proposals that still fail constraint. This compiler does not allow plausibility to substitute for admissibility.
